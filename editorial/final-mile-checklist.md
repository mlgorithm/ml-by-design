# Final-Mile Checklist

This document is the execution plan for taking **Solving AI Problems** from a strong manuscript to a publisher-ready, teachable book.

Packaging, positioning, and the first typography pass are already done. The remaining work is not a new structural rewrite. It is the last layer that makes the book feel complete, adoptable, and professionally finished.

## 10/10 Definition

The manuscript is at a true final stage when all of the following are true:

1. The book has one visible teaching spine.
   The seven-question framework and the recurring support-assistant case should feel cumulative rather than episodic.
2. Every chapter is teachable.
   Each chapter should have a clear exercise ladder and at least a minimal instructor-facing teaching path.
3. The visuals carry weight.
   Figures should make workflows, evaluation logic, reliability checks, and system architecture easier to remember.
4. The manuscript has survived outside pressure.
   At least three external readers should stress-test the book from different angles.
5. The production layer is clean.
   Terminology, artifact naming, references, glossary/index seeds, and PDF presentation should feel deliberate.

## Two-Week Plan

### Week 1: Teachability and Review Setup

1. Exercise architecture for all chapters
   Deliverable: a chapter-by-chapter exercise grid with three tiers:
   - quick checks
   - applied problems
   - one synthesis task
   Deliverable: a short hints policy and solutions policy for instructors.
   Acceptance check: every chapter from 1 to 12 has a visible exercise ladder, even if some solutions remain instructor-only drafts.

2. Figure and diagram system
   Deliverable: a prioritized figure list with target chapters and figure purpose.
   Deliverable: a small visual style guide covering captions, labels, and diagram conventions.
   Acceptance check: Chapters 2, 7, 8, 10, 11, and 12 each have at least one anchor figure that carries a core idea, not just decoration.

3. External review packet
   Deliverable: a reviewer packet with:
   - one-paragraph book summary
   - target audience statement
   - specific chapters to read
   - focused review questions
   Deliverable: a short list of three reviewer types:
   - one machine learning expert
   - one systems or reliability person
   - one target undergraduate reader or instructor
   Acceptance check: the review packet is ready to send without additional writing, and the first outreach wave is scheduled for March 17, 2026.

### Week 2: Finish the Adoption Layer

4. Instructor materials
   Deliverable: a 10 to 12 week sample syllabus.
   Deliverable: chapter dependency notes and a suggested course path.
   Deliverable: a solutions policy that distinguishes public material from instructor-only support.
   Acceptance check: an instructor can understand how to teach the book without reverse-engineering the manuscript.

5. Copy, consistency, and terminology pass
   Deliverable: a terminology sheet covering the named reusable techniques and chapter artifacts.
   Deliverable: a short issue log for repeated phrasing, uneven chapter openings, and inconsistent labels.
   Acceptance check: the front half and back half use the same core vocabulary for the book's method.

6. References, glossary, and index seed
   Deliverable: a bibliography audit for missing, weak, or inconsistent citations.
   Deliverable: a starter glossary and an index term seed list.
   Acceptance check: the manuscript is ready for a final editorial pass rather than still being a draft with placeholder back matter.

7. Release-candidate PDF pass
   Deliverable: one clean build with:
   - front matter aligned with current positioning
   - visually consistent chapter openings
   - figure placement checked
   - table density checked
   - no new layout regressions introduced by late edits
   Acceptance check: the PDF reads like a near-final book, not a live working draft.

## Priority Order

If time is tight, do the work in this order:

1. exercises and instructor materials
2. figure system
3. external review packet and dated review cycle
4. copy and terminology pass
5. references, glossary, and index seed

This order matters because the book is already differentiated. The biggest remaining risk is not originality. It is adoptability.

## Stop Conditions

Do not loop forever on vague polish. The manuscript is ready to move forward once:

1. every chapter has a credible exercise ladder
2. the back-half systems arc is matched by equally deliberate visuals
3. three external readers have been contacted on schedule, and at least two have responded
4. the teaching package exists in usable form
5. the remaining issues are copyediting-scale rather than structural

At that point, the right move is a final editorial pass, not more internal reinvention.
