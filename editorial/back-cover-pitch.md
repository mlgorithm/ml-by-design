## Back-Cover Copy

Most introductory machine-learning books do one of two things: they catalog methods, or they teach a software stack. **Solving AI Problems** teaches the full modeling arc instead. It starts with a real decision, turns that decision into a defensible learning task, asks what data and representation make the problem visible, insists on a serious baseline, and then asks what evidence actually justifies the result.

The book covers the machine-learning core of modern AI, from linear models and trees to neural networks, transfer, vision, language, and sequential data. But it is deliberately a machine-learning textbook, not a broad survey of search, planning, robotics, and reinforcement learning. It does not stop at model choice. It treats experiments, failure analysis, reliability, and system readiness as core technical material rather than late appendices. Written for undergraduates, instructors, and serious self-learners, it combines mathematical bridges, challenge exercises, and companion code with a strong emphasis on evidence, reliability, and real-system thinking.
