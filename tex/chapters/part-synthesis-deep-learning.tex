\chapter*{Part Synthesis: Deep Learning}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Part Synthesis: Deep Learning}

\begin{whychaptermatters}
This part widened the modeling story from engineered structure to learned and reusable structure. Students should now be able to debug a small neural workflow, separate optimization from generalization trouble, and say what changed when transfer, probing, retrieval, or tuning improved the result.
\end{whychaptermatters}

\begin{chaptersummary}
\item \textbf{Question this part taught.} When should structure be learned rather than engineered, and when is a learned representation reusable enough to support later tasks honestly?
\item \textbf{Artifact chain this part added.} Minimum credible neural training report $\rightarrow$ reuse and adaptation plan.
\item \textbf{What a student can now do.} Train and diagnose a small network, then make a cleaner transfer claim by separating the reusable space, new evidence, and downstream adaptation depth.
\end{chaptersummary}
