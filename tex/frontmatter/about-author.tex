\chapter*{About the Author}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{About the Author}

Sam Urmian is a researcher at SLATE (Centre for the Science of Learning \& Technology) and a PhD student in the Algorithms Group at the Department of Informatics, University of Bergen, Norway. His research lies at the intersection of algorithms and artificial intelligence, combining theoretical computer science with practical questions in machine learning and data-driven systems. His current work focuses on synthetic data generation and federated learning, and his broader interests include optimization, model checking, automated theorem proving, and combinatorial problems.

Before this book, his published work spans width-based automated theorem proving, learnability of unitary branching programs, and supervised approaches to editorial article selection. He is also involved in organizing the AI Olympiad in Norway (NOKI), connecting students with AI, algorithms, and problem solving.

This book grew from a conviction that machine learning is often taught either as a catalog of methods or as a software toolkit, when it should be taught as a discipline of problem framing, evidence, and system design. The seven recurring questions that organize the text reflect the habits that separate reliable practice from score chasing.
